Questions, answered

Straight answers — no fine-print games.

Where does this data come from?

Every row comes from the Florida Department of State, Division of Corporations — the official public record of new business registrations. We watch those filings daily and hand you the freshest ones, cleaned up and tagged by industry.

Is this legal? It feels almost too good.

Yes. This is public-record business data that anyone can request — we just do the daily legwork for you. You run your own outreach your own way. We never autodial or text personal cell numbers, and the data is about businesses, not consumer credit.

How fresh is each Monday's list?

Very. The records update daily, and your sheet covers the businesses that registered the prior week. Last week there were 751 new businesses in Orange County — roughly 150 every business day — so there's always a fresh batch waiting for you on Monday morning.

What's actually in each row?

The business name, its industry, the city, the owner's name, and the mailing address. About 93% of rows include a real owner name and mailing address — the rest are brand-new filings still finalizing their paperwork.

Can I cancel whenever I want?

Absolutely. It's month-to-month — cancel anytime, no penalty, no awkward phone call. And if you're one of the first 5 founding members, your rate is locked for life for as long as you stay.

What happens if my county is already taken?

Because we only work with one insurance producer per county, that territory closes once it's claimed. If yours is already spoken for, we'll let you know and add you to the waitlist for the moment it opens back up. Neighboring counties like Seminole, Osceola, and Lake may still be open.
